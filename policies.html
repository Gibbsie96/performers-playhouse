Performers Playhouse Policies

Registration & Tuition

Tuition is due in full at the time of registration. A camper’s spot is not reserved until the registration form is complete and payment is received.

Refund & Cancellation Policy

Because camp space is limited, tuition is non-refundable once registration is complete.

If camp is canceled by the instructor or minimum enrollment is not met, families will receive a full refund or be given the opportunity to transfer to another available week.

Conduct & Behavior

Campers are expected to be kind, respectful, and safe. This includes respecting the instructor, other campers, the home studio, materials, personal space, and the belongings of others.

Campers must follow directions, stay with the group, use appropriate language, and participate safely in singing, acting, movement, theater games, and group activities.

Bullying, harassment, profanity, aggression, threats, unsafe physical behavior, repeated disruption, inappropriate language/content, leaving the group without permission, or behavior that makes others feel unsafe or uncomfortable will not be allowed.

First Behavioral Issue

The instructor will speak with the camper and the parent/guardian to address the concern and set expectations moving forward.

Second Behavioral Issue

If the behavior continues after the initial discussion, the camper may be removed from camp without refund at the instructor’s discretion.

Serious Behavioral Issue

Some behaviors may require immediate parent pickup or removal from camp without a prior warning. This includes physical aggression, threats, bullying, harassment, leaving the group or home studio without permission, property damage, or any behavior that creates a safety concern for the camper, other students, or the instructor.

Property Damage

Campers are expected to respect the property of others, as well as the home studio, equipment, props, instruments, and materials. Parents/guardians will be financially responsible for damage caused by negligence, intentional misuse, or unsafe behavior.

Support Needs

Parents/guardians must disclose any medical, behavioral, sensory, emotional, learning, social, or physical needs that may affect their child’s participation.

Reasonable efforts will be made to support each camper. However, this is a small-group performing arts camp and is not a one-on-one therapeutic, behavioral, or childcare program. If a camper needs support beyond what can safely be provided, the instructor may determine that camp is not the right fit.

Assumption of Risk

Participation in performing arts activities carries inherent risks, including singing, acting, movement, theater games, physical activity, use of props, and other related camp activities.

By registering, parents/guardians acknowledge and accept these risks and give permission for their child to participate. Parents/guardians release the instructor from liability for injuries or damages sustained during camp, except where prohibited by law.

Medical Release

If a camper becomes ill or injured, the instructor will attempt to contact the parent/guardian and emergency contacts listed on the registration form.

If they cannot be reached, the parents/guardians agree that the instructor may seek emergency medical care for the child, including calling 911 or authorizing treatment by medical professionals.

Parents/guardians are responsible for any medical expenses related to their child’s care.

Illness Policy

Please keep your child home if they have a fever, vomiting, diarrhea, contagious illness, unexplained rash, or symptoms that prevent full participation.

If a camper becomes ill during camp, a parent/guardian will be contacted for pickup. Missed camp time due to illness is not refundable.

Allergies, Snacks & Water

If bringing a snack, it should be labeled and nut-free.

Food sharing is not allowed.

Parents/guardians must disclose all allergies, dietary restrictions, medical needs, and emergency medication information during registration. Camp cannot guarantee an allergen-free environment.

Medication

Staff do not administer medication except authorized emergency medication, such as an inhaler or EpiPen.

Emergency medication must be labeled with the camper’s name and include clear instructions.

Authorized Pickup

Campers will only be released to a parent/guardian or authorized adult listed on the registration form unless written permission is provided by the parent/guardian. Authorized pickup adults may be asked to show photo ID.

If someone is legally prohibited from picking up the child, current legal documentation must be provided.

Late Pickup

Camp ends promptly at the scheduled dismissal time. A 5-minute grace period may be given.

After the 5-minute grace period, a late pickup fee of $1 per minute, per child may be charged until the camper is picked up.

Repeated late pickups may affect future camp enrollment.

Attendance

Because camp is short, consistent attendance matters.

If a camper misses a day, arrives late, or leaves early, their participation in the final showcase may be adjusted.

No refunds or credits are given for missed camp time.

Roles, Solos & Showcase

Camp will include a casual final showcase for families.

Each camper will be given an opportunity for a solo, speaking line, or featured moment if they wish.

Specific roles, solos, songs, lines, featured moments, and group assignments are chosen by the instructor based on age, readiness, attendance, vocal suitability, group needs, and camp structure.

Photo & Video

Parents/guardians may not publicly post photos or videos that include other campers without permission from those campers’ parents/guardians.

Phones & Devices

Phones and smartwatches must remain put away during camp unless the instructor gives permission.

Campers may not take photos, videos, or audio recordings during camp without permission.

Parents should contact the instructor directly for urgent communication during camp hours.

ESA Receipt / Reimbursement

Families who need an itemized receipt for ESA reimbursement may request one during registration.
